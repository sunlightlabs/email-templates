Lightning Talks on:
How Transparency Can Kick-Start Congress

Monday, January 28

16 open government, reform and technology experts to present at free event

The Advisory Committee on Transparency (ACT), a project of the Sunlight Foundation, will host a series of “lightning talks” on the topic of what Congress can do, right now, to make government more transparent. Hear their innovative ideas on Monday, Jan. 28 at 2 pm on Capitol Hill. The event is one of a continuing series that ACT hosts to address important and wide-ranging transparency policy issues with those in Congress, think tanks, the media and other experts.

Titled “Kick Starting the 113th Congress,” 16 speakers will deliver short, 3-minute presentations on concrete, actionable proposals to open up the government. This lively and engaging format, modeled after “Ignite Talks,” features a diverse line up of speakers across the political spectrum.

Experts from think tanks, universities and advocacy groups will cover topics such as FOIA requests, IRS disclosure rules, lobbying reform, open data availability and formats, public access to Congress Research Service reports and more.

This free event is open to the public and the media.
